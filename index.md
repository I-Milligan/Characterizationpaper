## Scientific Abstract:

Convergent evolution provides a framework for understanding how similar functional pressures shape biological systems across distantly related taxa. Although morphological convergence is well documented, less is known about the molecular evolution of complex physiological processes such as wound healing. Here, we conducted a comparative genomic analysis of the gelatinases MMP2 and MMP9 across 13 vertebrate species representing diverse ecological niches and healing strategies. Phylogenetic reconstruction, pairwise sequence divergence, and multiple sequence alignments revealed strong conservation of MMP2 across taxa, consistent with essential proteolytic function. In contrast, MMP9 displayed greater variability, particularly within regulatory and accessory domains. Several amino acid substitutions were uniquely shared among cetaceans, suggesting lineage-specific adaptations affecting extracellular matrix remodelling. These findings highlight contrasting evolutionary dynamics between MMP2 and MMP9 and identify candidate regions potentially associated with divergent wound-healing strategies.
